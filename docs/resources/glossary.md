# Glossary

Key terms used in Archa and DeFi.

## A

### APY (Annual Percentage Yield)
The real rate of return on an investment over one year, accounting for compound interest.

### Arisan
Traditional Indonesian rotating savings and credit association where members contribute regularly and take turns receiving the pot.

## B

### Blockchain
A distributed ledger technology that records transactions across many computers, making records immutable and transparent.

### Bridge
A protocol that allows transferring assets between different blockchain networks.

## C

### Collateral
An asset pledged as security for a loan or commitment. In Archa, USDC deposited to guarantee participation.

### Smart Contract
Self-executing code stored on a blockchain that automatically enforces agreed-upon rules.

## D

### DeFi (Decentralized Finance)
Financial services built on blockchain technology, operating without traditional intermediaries like banks.

### DEX (Decentralized Exchange)
A cryptocurrency exchange that operates without a central authority, using smart contracts.

## G

### Gas Fee
The cost to execute transactions on a blockchain network. Mantle has very low gas fees.

### Gotong Royong
Indonesian concept of communal cooperation and mutual assistance.

## L

### L2 (Layer 2)
A secondary framework built on top of an existing blockchain (like Ethereum) to improve scalability and reduce costs.

### Liquidity
The ease with which an asset can be converted to cash or another asset without affecting its price.

## M

### Mantle Network
An Ethereum L2 network optimized for low transaction costs and high throughput.

### MNT
The native token of Mantle Network, used for gas fees.

## P

### Pool
In Archa, a group of participants in a single arisan cycle with shared parameters.

### Protocol
A set of rules governing how data is transmitted and processed in a network or application.

## R

### Rotating Savings
A savings system where members take turns receiving the collected contributions.

## S

### Stablecoin
A cryptocurrency designed to maintain a stable value, typically pegged to a fiat currency like USD.

## T

### TVL (Total Value Locked)
The total amount of assets deposited in a DeFi protocol, often used as a measure of protocol health.

### Transaction
An action recorded on the blockchain, such as transferring tokens or interacting with a smart contract.

## U

### USDC
USD Coin, a stablecoin pegged 1:1 to the US dollar, issued by Circle.

## W

### Wallet
Software that stores cryptocurrency private keys and allows users to interact with blockchain networks.

### Web3
The next evolution of the internet built on blockchain technology, emphasizing decentralization and user ownership.

## Y

### Yield
The income generated from an investment, expressed as a percentage of the investment's value.

### Yield Farming
The practice of moving cryptocurrency between different DeFi protocols to maximize returns.
